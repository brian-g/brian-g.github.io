Plate Glass Shelf Plans

I needed a good shelf to put my equipment on so that my child would not play with it. I also wanted that shelf to look good at the same time. My room is kinda small and thus off the shelf shelfs were not an option either. My wife suggested that I put the reciever and DVD player on a shelf above the TV. Perfect I thought, now just to find the right material.

I didn't want wood because it wasn't elegant enough for a shelf that juts out 20". I looked at glass shelving, but the kits they sell in home stores can't really hold the weight I intended. I search the web using many a search term. One company offered hardware of the exact nature that I wanted, but the specs for the system limited the shelf depth to 16", 4 short of what I wanted. I toyed with the idea of exceeding the specs, because most hardware can probably exceed the capabilities of the spec, but I chose not to.

What I did find at this company, was a very attractive shelf bracket. An idea struck upon me to combine this bracket with cables to make a glass shelf that could carry the weight of the equipment with the size of glass I needed.

Parts

  • 1/4" plate glass 20" x 36", sanded edges, 1/4" holes drilled in two corners along the long side 2" in from each edge.
  • 3 Adjustable Shelf Bracket. Availble from Advance Glass for about $5.25 ea.
  • 19 gauge or higher steel wire, sold in 50 ft. reels.
  • 2 3/16" closed eyelets and bolts.
  • 2 washers
  • 2 1/4" lag bolt closed eyelets
  • Felt pads, self adhesive to act as a cushion between the glass and washers..
The plate glass should cost about $13.00-$20.00. Shops will charge extra depending upon the type of edge put on. They'll also charge you more for the holes. The adjustable shelf brackets will cost about $5.25 each plus about $4 for shipping. The rest of the parts will cost about $5-10 at most hardware stores.
